About Me

I am a civil engineer with a passion for solving complex engineering challenges through data-driven insights and cutting-edge technologies. My extensive hands-on experience spans a wide range of research projects, from pavement engineering to infrastructure risk management, where I have developed a strong foundation in combining technical expertise with innovative solutions. I specialize in applying programming, machine learning, signal processing, and advanced data analytics to improve civil infrastructure systems, contributing to enhanced efficiency, safety, and sustainability.

Throughout my career, I’ve worked on diverse projects, including road condition monitoring, pavement analysis, and structural health monitoring. I leverage advanced algorithms, big data analytics, and custom hardware systems to solve real-world problems, ensuring optimal performance and longevity of infrastructure. My tenure at the Department of Roads, Government of Nepal (2015-2024), provided me with significant experience in road and bridge construction, geotechnical investigations, and risk management. I was involved in the successful upgrading of national highways, local road networks, and bridges, where I applied my skills in risk assessment and management, construction monitoring, quality control, and bid evaluation to ensure quality and safety at every stage of the project. My technical expertise includes pavement design, surface dressing, slurry seal, semi-grout applications, and geotechnical engineering techniques such as slope stabilization, embankment construction, and sub-grade preparation. I am experienced in conducting geotechnical investigations and laboratory testing (including AIV, ACV, Proctor Compaction, and Field Density Assessments), as well as applying non-destructive testing (NDT) methods such as the Rebound Hammer and Pile Integrity tests. I have a strong focus on structural works, including road and bridge construction, shallow and deep foundation works, retaining walls, and geo-textile applications. My ability to manage complex construction projects while ensuring safety and adherence to quality standards has been a key strength in delivering impactful infrastructure solutions.

Beyond my technical skills, I am motivated by curiosity and a drive to find creative, efficient solutions. I thrive on bridging the gap between traditional civil engineering and digital innovation. As a data science enthusiast, I focus on integrating advanced technologies into infrastructure systems to enhance their resilience and sustainability. With expertise in software tools such as QGIS, SW-Road, SW-DTM, AUTOCAD, SAP-2000, and statistical tools like SPSS and Python, I am committed to leveraging modern technologies for smarter infrastructure development. Holding a strong academic background in Civil Engineering and Construction Management from Pokhara University, Nepal, I am dedicated to continuous learning and growth. By combining creativity, collaboration, and a results-oriented mindset, I aim to push the boundaries of civil engineering, contributing to the development of smarter, more resilient infrastructure systems.

Seismic analysis and design of earthquake resistance structural building

This project work was intended to learn the structural analysis and seismic resistant design of Residential building. It was mainly based on the structural analysis using SAP-2000 and manual design of structural elements for the analysis using different design codes as per requirement. In the analysis of seismic resistant design of the building, the negative moment in beam was greater than that of the positive moment, as a result of which, the under- reinforced design of beam made it inevitable to provide more reinforcement as a top bar than as a bottom bar. Hence, this high negative moment in beam which demanded high reinforcement on the top fiber, especially near the joints, was due to the earthquake consideration in the analysis and design of the structure. In the same way, the spacing of the lateral bars near the support and at the centre of the beam and column. More importantly, the behavior of structure with respect to the seismicity could be learned from the design of column. It was concluded that the columns in the periphery of the whole building are more critical to high moment and the columns in the central portion are more critical to high axial load. as we move one storey above, the axial load and moments developed therein are also reduced comparatively. As a result of which the demand of the reinforcement is also decreased comparatively. The analysis design and detailing of all structural members is required to complete the entire design but due to unavailability of time only calculation for the design of critical member is presented here. Rest of the member can be designed by following the same procedure.
